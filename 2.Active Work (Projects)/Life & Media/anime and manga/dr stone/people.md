### Senku = the protagonist you admire

Senku Ishigami is the character almost everyone wishes they could be. He has absurd knowledge, incredible memory, and can connect ideas across chemistry, physics, engineering, and biology.

But most people aren't Senku.

---

### Chrome = the relatable scientist

Chrome is arguably the most important scientist in the story.

Why?

Because Chrome starts with **curiosity**, not knowledge.

He sees a phenomenon and asks:

> "Why does that happen?"

That's the actual foundation of science.

Senku already knows thousands of answers.

Chrome learns how to discover answers.

Honestly, when I look at your interests—AI, homelabs, robotics, self-hosting, DevOps, low-level systems—you remind me more of Chrome than Senku.

You often jump into things because you're curious:

- "How does GitLab work internally?"
- "How do AI agents work?"
- "How do operating systems work?"
- "How does networking actually function?"

That's very Chrome-like.

---

### Sai = underrated genius

Sai Nanami is one of the most underrated characters.

I love that he represents a different kind of intelligence.

Senku:

- Scientist

Chrome:

- Explorer

Sai:

- Computational thinker

Give Sai:

- Math
- Logic
- Simulation
- Optimization

and he becomes terrifyingly effective.

For someone interested in AI and programming, Sai is actually one of the closest characters to a modern software engineer.

---

### Suika = the MVP nobody talks about enough

Suika is one of the strongest characters emotionally.

She starts as a kid trying to help.

But throughout the series she keeps doing difficult things even when nobody is watching.

One of the reasons fans love Suika is that she doesn't have:

- Super strength
- Genius intellect
- Special powers

She just keeps trying.

And somehow ends up saving the day multiple times.

---

### Funny thing about your favorites

They form a pretty complete team:

|Character|Represents|
|---|---|
|Senku|Knowledge|
|Chrome|Curiosity|
|Sai|Logic|
|Suika|Persistence|

And honestly, if I had to pick which one best matches the way you've approached learning over the last couple of years, it'd be:

**Chrome + Sai.**

Chrome because you keep wandering into new fields and asking "how does this actually work?"

Sai because you're increasingly interested in building systems—AI agents, programming, automation, infrastructure, and understanding the underlying logic rather than just using tools.

The good news is that real engineers usually don't become Senku.

They become Chrome first.

Then they slowly accumulate enough knowledge that, years later, people assume they were always smart. 😄
